# Client Reporting Prompts

---

## 1. Monthly Performance Summary

**When to use:** Turning raw metrics into a readable client update.

```
I have the following marketing performance data for [CLIENT NAME] for [MONTH]:

[PASTE DATA — traffic, leads, rankings, impressions, conversions, etc.]

Write a client-facing monthly summary that:
1. Opens with the 2-3 most important things that happened
2. Explains what drove the results (not just what the numbers were)
3. Flags anything that needs attention
4. States what we're doing next month and why

Tone: professional but clear — no jargon. The reader is a busy founder or marketing director.
Keep it under 300 words.
```

---

## 2. Data → Insight Converter

**When to use:** When you have numbers but need to turn them into a recommendation.

```
Here is some marketing data:
[PASTE DATA]

Analyze this and tell me:
- What's working and why
- What's underperforming and the likely reason
- One concrete action I should take based on this data
- One thing I should watch closely next month

Be specific. Don't just describe what the numbers say — tell me what they mean.
```

---

## 3. Client Status Email

**When to use:** Weekly or biweekly updates to keep clients informed.

```
Write a short client status email for [CLIENT NAME].

Updates to include:
- [BULLET 1]
- [BULLET 2]
- [BULLET 3]

Upcoming work:
- [NEXT STEPS]

Tone: confident, proactive, not defensive. 
Format: short intro → bullets of what happened → what's next → any questions/decisions needed from client.
Max 200 words.
```

---

## 4. Meeting Recap

**When to use:** After a client call — turning notes into a clean summary.

```
Turn these raw call notes into a clean meeting recap:

[PASTE NOTES]

Format:
- Date + attendees
- Key decisions made
- Action items (with owner and deadline if mentioned)
- Open questions / follow-ups
- Any important context from the conversation

Keep it scannable. The goal is a record both sides can refer back to.
```
