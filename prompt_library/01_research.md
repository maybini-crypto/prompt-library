# Research Prompts

---

## 1. Company / Competitor Deep Dive

**When to use:** Before a client call, building a competitive brief, or understanding a new market.

```
You are a B2B marketing analyst. Research [COMPANY NAME] and give me:
1. What they do and who they sell to (ICP)
2. Their key differentiators vs. competitors
3. How they position themselves in their market
4. What marketing channels they appear to be investing in
5. Any notable recent news, product launches, or partnerships

Be specific. Avoid generic descriptions. If you're unsure about something, say so.
```

---

## 2. Topic Landscape Map

**When to use:** Starting a content strategy for a new client or entering a new niche.

```
I'm building a content strategy for a B2B company in [INDUSTRY/NICHE].

Map out the topic landscape:
- Core topics this audience cares about
- Sub-topics under each
- Questions buyers ask at each stage (awareness, consideration, decision)
- Topics that are oversaturated vs. underserved

Format as a structured outline I can use to plan content.
```

---

## 3. Audience Intelligence

**When to use:** Understanding a client's target buyer before writing content or ads.

```
My client sells [PRODUCT/SERVICE] to [TARGET ROLE] at [COMPANY TYPE].

Help me understand this buyer:
- What are their top 3 daily frustrations?
- What do they care about in a vendor?
- What objections would they have?
- What language do they use to describe their problems?
- Where do they go to find information and stay current?

Be specific and practical. Base this on what you know about this persona type.
```

---

## 4. Quick News Briefing

**When to use:** Staying current on a client's industry before a call or update.

```
Give me a briefing on what's happening in [INDUSTRY] right now.
Focus on:
- Trends that are gaining momentum
- Anything that's disrupting the status quo
- What smart companies in this space are doing differently

Keep it concise — 5 bullet points max, each with a one-line "so what" for a marketer.
```
