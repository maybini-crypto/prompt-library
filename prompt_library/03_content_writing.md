# Content Writing Prompts

---

## 1. Blog Post Draft

**When to use:** Generating a first draft from a brief or outline.

```
Write a blog post based on this brief:

Title: [TITLE]
Audience: [WHO IS READING]
Goal: [WHAT SHOULD THEY THINK/DO AFTER READING]
Key points to cover: [LIST 3-5 POINTS]
Tone: [e.g. direct and practical / conversational but expert / formal B2B]
Word count: [TARGET]

Important:
- Open with a hook, not a definition or "In today's world..."
- Use subheadings every 2-3 paragraphs
- Include at least one concrete example or analogy
- End with a clear takeaway or CTA
```

---

## 2. Rewrite for Clarity & Punch

**When to use:** Cleaning up draft content that's too wordy or generic.

```
Rewrite the following content to be clearer and more impactful.

Original:
[PASTE CONTENT]

Rules:
- Cut any sentence that doesn't add information
- Replace vague language ("leverage", "utilize", "solutions") with specific words
- Make the opening sentence stronger — it should grab attention
- Keep the same meaning and core message
- Match this tone: [DESCRIBE TONE e.g. confident, direct, not salesy]
```

---

## 3. Repurpose Into LinkedIn Post

**When to use:** Turning a blog post or article into a LinkedIn post.

```
Turn this content into a LinkedIn post:

[PASTE CONTENT]

Format:
- Hook in the first line (no "I'm excited to share..." — get to the point)
- 3-5 short punchy paragraphs or a tight numbered list
- End with a question or clear CTA
- Tone: [first-person / thought leadership / conversational]
- Max 250 words
```

---

## 4. Ad Copy Variants

**When to use:** Writing Google or LinkedIn ad copy for a client campaign.

```
Write 3 variants of ad copy for this campaign:

Product/service: [WHAT ARE WE PROMOTING]
Audience: [WHO WILL SEE THIS AD]
Main benefit: [WHAT'S THE #1 THING THEY SHOULD KNOW]
CTA: [e.g. Book a demo / Download the guide / Get started]
Platform: [Google / LinkedIn]
Character limits: Headline [30 chars] / Description [90 chars]

Make each variant test a different angle:
- Variant A: Lead with the pain point
- Variant B: Lead with the outcome/benefit
- Variant C: Lead with credibility or social proof
```

---

## 5. Email Outreach Draft

**When to use:** Cold or warm outreach emails for client campaigns or partnerships.

```
Write a short outreach email:

Sender context: [WHO IS SENDING AND WHY]
Recipient: [ROLE / COMPANY TYPE]
Goal of the email: [WHAT DO YOU WANT THEM TO DO]
Key hook: [WHY SHOULD THEY CARE — BE SPECIFIC]

Rules:
- Subject line: under 8 words, curiosity-driven but not clickbait
- Body: 3-4 sentences max
- No buzzwords. Sound like a human.
- Clear, single CTA in the last line
```
